About me

I've been a digital designer since 1998, when I was first exposed to Bryce and Adobe Photoshop 3. Originally a Computer Information Systems major, I stumbled into a digital art class that steered me toward graphic and motion design.

In the early aughts I picked up HTML and CSS from an interest in understanding how things work behind the scenes — something I still find satisfaction in. I also started working with self-hosted WordPress websites and eventually started a lucrative business.

Evolution

Around 2016, I starting feeling like I couldn't do any more of the same thing. So I learned more about user experience, and expanded that into design thinking. Finally, I had terms that defined what was important to me the whole time — to build the solution for the user, not the maker; to find solutions that are viable and desired.

In April, 2018 I completed the Akendi CXD UX Design Certification program in Montréal, Canada, where I learned the critical importance of user research. I then decided I wanted to work alongside a researcher. I got hired on an experience team that has both testing and a11y consulting capabilities, which is where I am today.

During the winter of 2019, I completed Portland State University's Design Thinking and Innovation program. And in February 2020 I began the self-paced coursework at Deque to become a Certified Professional in Web Accessibility (CPWA).

Play time

Outside of work, and when we aren't in quarantine, I enjoy hiking, snowshoeing and exploring the Pacific Northwest. Last year I hiked over 350 miles, including summiting Mt St. Helens with some friends.
