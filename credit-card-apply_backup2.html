U.S. Bank retail credit card application

The purpose of this project is to take U.S. Bank's retail partner credit card application experience into the modern era. This responsive web experience is built by the bank and consumed by over 2,000 retail and co-brand partners, so consistency and customization are essential.

Project kickoff

There are several core goals of this design effort.

  1. First, reduce the time a user spends filling out a digital application for an instant line of credit.
  2. Second, make the entire experience accessible.
  3. Third, provide a modern, consistent, and customizable platform for partners and co-brands.

For perspective, the following image is the original application form.

The original credit card application form for partners and co-brands. At first glance one can see some usability and accessibility issues.

To kickoff the project, a design sprint was held with stakeholders. Afterward, my role was to amalgamate the voted-for design ideas and create low-fidelity screens for a prototype we would test in a usability study. I collaborated with the customer experience and research teams to define the parameters for the test.

A snapshot of the InVision Freehand board used for the design sprint sketches.

Low-fidelity screens used in the test prototype.

Wireframes

After some conversations, compromises and tweaks based on our usability test results, I wireframed the first release MVP product in Axure. I have studied hundreds of pages of business and validation rules on the current state application platform to understand the various products, partner nuances, and data mapping. Since the project's inception, I have created and managed over 50 wireframes for 80+ features, which include functional, interaction, and accessibility rules. My time is split between the refinement of "work-ahead" features, and supporting three development teams on their current work.

Wireframes in Axure.

Visual Design

For the UI, I began with assembling a spinoff set of patterns from the core U.S. Bank design system. Because our group is not an omnichannel team -- rather, we support the bank's retail partners, I could not simply use the USB design system alone. There were a number of considerations on what would be the best set of patterns for our platform and our partners -- accessibility, mobile-first design, and the much-needed ability to customize to the partners' brands. The UI pattern set you see here is a result of this work. It is a Sketch symbol library that evolves along with me on this journey.

The pattern library I designed for this project.
